-A Love Poem from My Shopping Mall-

Roses are red,
Violets are blue,
Comments are hilarious,
And so are you.

Orchids are white,
Ghost ones are rare,
The path is straight,
And so is your hair.

Magnolia grows,
With buds like eggs,
Lips are thin,
And so are your legs.

Sunflowers reach,
Up to the skies,
Algae are green,
And so are your eyes.

Foxgloves in hedges,
Surround the farms,
The eagle is bald,
And so are your arms.

Daisies are pretty,
Daffies have style,
An atmosphere is friendly,
And so is your smile.

A moonlight is beautiful,
Just like you.

One day at a grocery shop,
I met a man selling apples,
For money he wanted to swap,
But I really wanted some pineapples.

"Got any pineapples?" asked I.
"For that's how I'll spend my money."
"No pineapples here!" said the guy.
He seemed to find it quite funny.

"We've got some lovely pies,
I'll give you a very fine price."
"I'd rather have some ties."
The man blinked rapidly thrice.

The man seemed exceptionally cute,
And his manner was strangely amused.
He wasn't what I would call subacute,
Great disdain he noticeably oozed.

Like others, he thought I was odd,
Some say I'm a bit grumpy.
Still he gave me a courteous nod,
As if he thought I was plenty bumpy.

So in search of my goal I departed,
But before the grocery shop could I leave,
The man came running full-hearted,
"I can help you I believe."

"Apples, pineapples, you shall find.
Pies, ties, you can get.
You must now open your mind,
